## PART 1: INDIVIDUAL SCENARIOS (1-25)

### Scenario 1: The Burned-Out Expert Regressing
**Context:** Physics professor (15 years tenure) who designed award-winning curriculum; now teaching online due to pandemic burnout.

**Observable Behavior:**
- Can still solve advanced quantum mechanics problems fluently (E6-L6 level work)
- No longer prepares novel demonstrations; reuses decade-old lectures verbatim
- When asked about pedagogical innovation: "I used to care about this. I don't anymore."
- Refuses to engage with new learning management systems ("I'll figure it out if forced")
- Students report: clear explanations, zero engagement with their questions beyond minimum requirements

**LVA Diagnosis:**
- **Engagement:** L3 (Compliant) — performs job requirements, internal motivation absent
- **Capability:** L6 (Fluent) — deep procedural mastery, intuitive grasp of domain
- **Portability:** L4 (Self-Directed) — can articulate physics clearly, won't articulate pedagogy anymore

**MECE Stress Point:**
The framework assumes progression is generally monotonic. Here we see **regression in Engagement while Capability remains high**. The professor still *has* generative capability (L7 potential) but Engagement collapse prevents its deployment. This tests whether the framework can account for motivational decoupling from competence. Is this a violation of MECE or a valid configuration showing the dimensions are truly independent?

**LVA Diagnosis:**
- **Engagement:** L4 (Strategic) — calculates credential ROI, believes in future applicability

- **Portability:** L2 (Institution-Legible) — credentials are visible, but no demonstrated ability to mobilize them

**MECE Stress Point:**
High Portability (credentials are hyper-visible) masks low Capability (nothing to actually port). The scenario tests whether Portability can be meaningful without real Capability beneath it. Is this a framework gap—should Portability require minimum Capability threshold? Or should Portability L2 (Institution-Legible) be recognized as decoupled from demonstrated competence?

### Scenario 3: The Jazz Improviser
**Context:** Self-taught saxophonist who learned entirely by ear, no formal training; performs regularly in clubs.

**Observable Behavior:**
- Can improvise fluently, adapts to other musicians in real-time
- Cannot read sheet music; avoids formal jazz theory study
- When asked "What scale are you using?": nonplussed; "I just feel it"
- Mentors younger musicians by saying "Listen, then copy, then make it yours"
- No ability to explain harmonic structure despite intuitive mastery

**LVA Diagnosis:**
- **Engagement:** L5 (Committed) — jazz is identity; invested in continuous improvement
- **Capability:** L6 (Fluent) — generates novel improvisations, adapts intuitively
- **Portability:** L1 (Tacit) — cannot articulate knowledge; framework is invisible to them

**MECE Stress Point:**
This is the classic tacit-knowledge paradox: high Capability (L6) paired with low Portability (L1). The framework's definition of Portability assumes the learner can eventually articulate or externalize knowledge. But tacit mastery may be *fundamentally* resistant to articulation without destroying the intuitive flow. Can true L1 Tacit coexist with L6 Fluent Capability? Or does this reveal that Portability levels need subcategories for "articulate-able" vs. "inarticulable" knowledge?

A learner at L6 Capability should naturally have higher Portability, but environmental friction prevents it. Should the framework distinguish between "can't articulate knowledge" (learner-internal) vs. "can't communicate knowledge" (system-external)? This reveals potential MECE overlap: Portability and Engagement both depend on communication ability.

**MECE Stress Point:**
AI creates a new form of artificial capability that inflates apparent Capability and Portability levels. The learner appears L3-L4 when augmented, L1-L2 when not. Does the framework need a dimension for "autonomy of capability" or "capability dependence"? Is a Capability genuine if it's contingent on external tools? Where does AI-augmentation belong in the model—as a legitimate form of capability (since humans always use tools) or as scaffolding masking actual capability level?

### Team Scenario 1: The High-Performing Dysfunctional Team

- Process improvement: L2-L3 (never implemented)

A team's Capability is not the sum of individuals' capabilities. Team-level learning requires Engagement in team processes that may conflict with individual-level Engagement in technical work. ### Team Scenario 2: The New Team with Expert Hire
**Context:** Early-stage team hires a senior expert as technical lead.

**Observable Behavior:**
- Expert: L6-L7 (Generative) in domain

- Expert engagement in teaching: L2 (Disoriented) — "Why do I have to explain this?"

- **Expert Capability:** L6-L7 (Generative)
- **Expert Portability:** L5-L6 (Translated) — can explain, doesn't want to
- **Team Engagement:** L2-L3 (Disoriented-Compliant) in learning; depends on expert
- **Team Capability:** L2-L3 (Inert-Imitative) — can copy but not understand
- **Team Portability:** L1-L2 (Tacit-Institution-Legible) — knowledge is in expert, not distributed

**MECE Stress Point:**
Expert presence can suppress team learning through multiple mechanisms: (1) team doesn't need to engage in sense-making, (2) expert's low teaching engagement, (3) team's dependent Engagement. This tests whether high individual capability enables or inhibits team capability growth. Should the framework include a "knowledge transfer" variable that moderates individual-to-team scaling?

- **Portability:** L5-L6 (Translated-Integrated) — knowledge is distributed, articulated, learnable by new members

**MECE Stress Point:**
A team can achieve higher collective Capability than individual members warrant. Engagement in learning processes drives Portability (knowledge distribution) which amplifies team Capability. ### Team Scenario 4: The Knowledge Hoarding Culture
**Context:** Organization where knowledge sharing is discouraged (job security fears, competitive internal dynamics).

## PART 5: REVISED MECE BOUNDARY HYPOTHESIS

Based on the 70 scenarios, the framework requires boundary clarifications:

**Hypothesis 1: Dimensions are orthogonal but modulated by context**
Engagement, Capability, and Portability are independent, but external factors (environment, identity safety, cognitive load, modality) moderate their relationship and expression.

**Hypothesis 2: Scaling requires a "coherence" variable**
Individual-to-collective scaling isn't additive. Teams require a separate assessment of collective learning processes, knowledge distribution, and interdependence.

**Hypothesis 3: Portability isn't binary across modalities**
A learner can be at different Portability levels in different modalities (oral, written, embodied, relational). Portability should be modality-qualified.

**Hypothesis 4: Tacit knowledge has ceilings**
Some domains may have irreducible tacit components where Portability L6-L7 is unreachable. This should be domain-documented, not treated as individual learner failure.

**Hypothesis 5: Metacognitive accuracy is a separate axis**
Whether a learner accurately perceives their own capability matters for Engagement (self-belief affects continuation) and for Portability (overconfidence risks).

**Hypothesis 6: Environment is mediating variable, not dependent**
Psychological safety, cognitive load, learning structure, organizational design actively suppress or enable all three dimensions. These should be explicitly modeled.

**Hypothesis 7: Time-dependent trajectories matter**
Progression rates, regression risks, consolidation timelines, and dormancy effects vary across learners and contexts. Pure state classification is incomplete without trajectory modeling.

## CONCLUSION

The LVA framework successfully identifies three largely orthogonal dimensions of learning value, but MECE boundaries break down in 12 critical areas:

1. **Context-dependence** within single learners
2. **Tacit knowledge paradoxes** (high Capability, low Portability in some domains)
3. **Emotional/identity suppressors** of Engagement and Portability
4. **Environmental mediation** stronger than individual properties
5. **Scaling failures** from individual to collective
6. **Modality effects** on Capability/Portability manifestation
7. **Metacognitive misalignment** between self-perception and actual capability
8. **Relational emergence** that can't be decomposed to individual levels
9. **Autonomy effects** on learning quality despite high external structure
10. **Temporal dynamics** (progression, plateau, regression) not captured in state models
11. **Knowledge persistence** and organizational memory as separate from individual learning
12. **Credential-knowledge decoupling** where institutional signaling diverges from capability

**Recommendation:** Evolve MECE framework to include context-moderation variables (psychological safety, cognitive load, learning structure), modality-specific Capability/Portability sub-dimensions, explicit team/organizational scaling rules, and time-dependent trajectory models.
